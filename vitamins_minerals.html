Vitamins & Minerals and Their Functions

Fat-Soluble Vitamins
  • Vitamin A: Vision, immunity, reproduction, cell communication
  • Vitamin D: Calcium absorption, bone health, immune function
  • Vitamin E: Antioxidant, protects cells from damage
  • Vitamin K: Blood clotting, bone metabolism
Water-Soluble Vitamins
  • Vitamin C: Antioxidant, collagen synthesis, immune support, iron absorption
  • Vitamin B1 (Thiamine): Energy metabolism, nerve function
  • Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin): Energy production, skin and eye health
  • Vitamin B3 (Niacin): Metabolism, DNA repair, skin health
  • Vitamin B5 (Pantothenic Acid): Fatty acid synthesis, energy metabolism
  • Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine): Protein metabolism, brain development, immune function
  • Vitamin B7 (Biotin): Metabolism of fats, carbs, proteins; hair and nails
  • Vitamin B9 (Folate): DNA synthesis, red blood cell formation, fetal development
  • Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin): Nerve function, red blood cell formation, DNA synthesis
Major Minerals
  • Calcium: Bone and teeth health, muscle function, nerve signaling
  • Phosphorus: Bone formation, energy production (ATP)
  • Potassium: Fluid balance, muscle contractions, nerve signals
  • Sodium: Fluid balance, nerve and muscle function
  • Magnesium: Muscle and nerve function, blood glucose control
  • Chloride: Fluid balance, stomach acid production
  • Sulfur: Protein structure, enzyme function
Trace Minerals
  • Iron: Oxygen transport (hemoglobin), energy production
  • Zinc: Immune function, wound healing, cell division
  • Iodine: Thyroid hormone production
  • Selenium: Antioxidant, thyroid function
  • Copper: Iron metabolism, nervous system function
  • Manganese: Bone formation, metabolism
  • Fluoride: Teeth and bone strength
  • Chromium: Enhances insulin action, glucose metabolism
  • Molybdenum: Enzyme function, detoxification
